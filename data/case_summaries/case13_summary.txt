**Case Name:**  
Abdul S Attar v. Mst. Zahida Parveen and Others  

**Court:**  
Sindh High Court  

**Year:**  
1991  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the respondents were guilty of an offence under the Offence of Zina Ordinance, 1979, for contracting a fresh marriage before the iddat period, given that the dissolution decree issued by the Family Court was not yet effective under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Muslim Family Laws Ordinance (VIII of 1961) – Sections 7, 8, 21  
- Family Courts Act (West Pakistan) – Section 21  
- Offence of Zina Ordinance (VII of 1979) – Section 10(2)  
- Penal Code – Sections 494 and 109  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Petitioner and respondent married in 1981.  
2. Respondent filed a suit for dissolution in 1988; the suit and its appeal were dismissed.  
3. A constitutional petition remanded the matter to the Family Court; a decree of dissolution was issued on 30‑4‑1989.  
4. Petitioner challenged the decree in a constitutional petition (outcome not specified).  
5. Respondent contracted a new marriage on 24‑5‑1989 before the iddat period and filed a complaint under Section 10(2) of the Offence of Zina Ordinance.  
6. The Sessions Judge ordered a preliminary enquiry; the complaint was dismissed on 23‑11‑1989.  
7. A criminal revision application was filed and dismissed on 7‑2‑1990.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The criminal revision application was dismissed. The Court held that the dissolution decree was not effective until notice was served to the Chairman and a period of 90 days had elapsed; delayed service does not render the decree ineffective. Consequently, the respondents were not guilty of the offence under the Offence of Zina Ordinance.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
A dissolution decree under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance becomes effective only after notice is served to the Chairman and 90 days have passed; delayed service does not invalidate the decree, and revocation of a talaq can occur after such notice. Criminal liability under the Offence of Zina Ordinance depends on the effective status of the dissolution decree.
