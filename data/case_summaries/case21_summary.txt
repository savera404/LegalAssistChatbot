**Case Name:**  
Kaura and Others Petitioners v. Allah Ditta and Others Respondents  

**Court:**  
Lahore High Court  

**Year:**  
1999  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the petitioners are entitled to a one‑sixth share in the inheritance of Lakhu and whether the subsequent mutations of the property were valid, in light of customary law and the limitation period.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Civil Procedure Code (1908), Order XIV, Rule 1; Order VII, Rule 3; Section 115; Section 99  
- Specific Relief Act (1877), Section 42  
- Limitation Act (1908), Article 120  
- West Pakistan Land Revenue Act (1967), Section 42  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- The property (100 Kanals 5 Marlas) in Dera Ghazi Khan was originally owned by Walia, whose sons Lakhu, Pheri, and Masso held equal shares.  
- Lakhu died in 1944; his share was transferred to his heirs and subsequently to Pheri and Masso.  
- In 1992, Taggi (plaintiff, later deceased) sued for a one‑sixth share, claiming to be Lakhu’s daughter.  
- The trial court granted Taggi the share; the appellate court dismissed the appeal and affirmed the decree.  
- Petitioners (defendants) filed a civil revision under Section 115, challenging the issues framed and the application of custom and limitation.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Lahore High Court dismissed the civil revision petition and affirmed the earlier decree, holding that the trial court’s decision was sustained.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
A trial court must frame issues strictly in accordance with the objections and pleadings of the parties; failure to do so vitiates the decision and renders it unsustainable.
