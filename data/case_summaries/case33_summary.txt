**Case Name:** Kaneez Fatima, etc. Versus Senior Civil Judge, etc.  
**Court:** Lahore High Court, Judicial Department  
**Year:** 2024  

**Legal Issue:** Whether a court may order DNA testing for paternity determination in a civil case without the consent of the parties involved.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Articles 9 and 14 of the Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan (right to liberty and privacy).  
- Article 129(g) of the Qanun‑e‑Shahadat Order, 1984 (adverse inference when evidence is withheld).  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioners challenged an order dated 17 Oct 2022 by the Senior Civil Judge (Family Division), Hafizabad, directing DNA analysis of minor Umme Mabad (alias Maryam) to resolve a paternity dispute.  
- The order was based on a prior direction by this Court in Writ Petition No.16871‑H of 2022.  
- Petitioners argue DNA testing requires consent; respondents initially argued DNA is the best method but later conceded evidence was largely complete.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Court held that consent of the parties is essential for DNA testing in civil paternity matters. In the absence of consent, the court may draw an adverse inference under Article 129(g) of the Qanun‑e‑Shahadat Order and decide the paternity issue based on the evidence presented by the parties.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
DNA testing for paternity in civil cases cannot be compelled without the parties’ consent; lack of consent permits the court to infer adverse presumption and rely on other evidence.
