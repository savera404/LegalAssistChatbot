**Case Name:**  
YASIR KALWAR v. Mst. FARZEEN and others  

**Court:**  
Sindh High Court  

**Year:**  
2024  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the mother’s second marriage to a stranger disqualifies her from custody of her minor daughters under the Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, and whether custody should be transferred to the father.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 – Section 25 (application for custody)  
- Principles of Muhammadan Law – paragraphs 352 and 354 (effects of a mother’s second marriage on custody)  
- Concept of Hazmat/Haznat (mother’s right to custody until puberty)  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Petitioner YASIR KALWAR applied under Section 25 for custody of his minor daughters, Baby Iman (≈16/17 yrs) and Baby Aaira (≈13/14 yrs), after respondent Mst. FARZEEN contracted a second marriage with a stranger not related within the prohibited degree.  
2. Respondent argued that petitioner, a CSS officer posted in various cities, could not provide stable custody.  
3. Petitioner’s prior applications were dismissed; this petition followed the second marriage.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Court allowed the petition, set aside the earlier orders, and transferred custody of the minor daughters to the petitioner. The mother is granted visitation rights (at least once a week or as mutually agreed).  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
When a mother contracts a second marriage with a stranger not related within the prohibited degree, she forfeits her right to custody of her minor daughters; custody may be transferred to the father unless exceptional circumstances justify otherwise. The welfare of the child and the best‑interest test are paramount in determining custody.
