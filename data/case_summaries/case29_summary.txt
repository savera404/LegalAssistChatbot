**Case Name:** Civil Petition No. 616‑K of 2025 (against order of the High Court of Sindh, Circuit Court, Larkana)  

**Court:** Supreme Court of Pakistan (Appellate Jurisdiction)  

**Year:** 2025  

**Legal Issue:** Whether a divorced daughter of a deceased government servant is entitled to a family pension after the death of the pensioner, in light of Circular No. FD(PCDC)3(225)/2022.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Sindh Civil Servants Act, 1973 – Section 20 (pension entitlement upon death).  
- West Pakistan Civil Services Pension Rules, 1963 – Rule 4.10 (family pension).  
- Circular No. FD(PCDC)3(225)/2022 dated 5 Dec 2022 (clarifying eligibility of daughters).  
- Constitution of Pakistan, Article 9 (right to life, including access to pension).  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Father, a government servant, died 12 Feb 2002.  
- Respondent, unmarried at the time of death, received the father’s family pension until her marriage in 2022.  
- After obtaining a divorce (khula) in August 2022, Respondent sought resumption of the pension.  
- Petitioners denied the request citing the 2022 Circular.  
- The High Court granted the pension to Respondent.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Supreme Court held that the 2022 Circular is contrary to the Act and the Rules, as it imposes an unwarranted condition on marital status at the time of the pensioner’s death. Accordingly, Respondent, as a divorced daughter, is entitled to the family pension.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Entitlement to a family pension for a daughter of a deceased government servant is not limited by her marital status at the time of the pensioner’s death; circulars that impose such a condition are invalid.
