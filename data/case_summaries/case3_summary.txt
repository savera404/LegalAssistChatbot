**Case Name:**  
Shaukat Hussain v. Ms. Rubina and Others  

**Court:**  
Sindh High Court  

**Year:**  
1989  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the decree of dissolution of marriage, dower, and maintenance awarded by the 9th Family Judge was void because the petitioner allegedly failed to give notice of divorce and was denied natural justice.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Constitution of Pakistan, 1973 – Art. 199, Art. 227(1), Art. 268, Art. 2‑A  
- Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961 – s. 7  
- Code of Civil Procedure – Sec. 151, Sec. 12(2)  
- Legal Practitioners and Bar Councils Act, 1973  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioner married respondent on 7 May 1986; two children were born.  
- Petitioner left for Oman, returned 21 July 1988; alleged abuse of respondent.  
- Respondent filed divorce application 30 March 1988; petitioner orally pronounced divorce 18 March 1988.  
- 9th Family Judge decreed dissolution on 22 Feb 1989, ordered dower Rs 20,000 and maintenance Rs 1,000/month for three months.  
- Petitioner appealed and filed a constitutional petition claiming fraud, lack of notice, and denial of natural justice.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The constitutional petition was dismissed in limine. The decree of dissolution, dower, and maintenance was upheld. The court found that the petitioner was present at all hearings, had the opportunity to cross‑examine, and that the oral divorce was valid under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
A constitutional petition under Art. 199 cannot be entertained on the basis of alleged counsel misconduct or lack of notice if the petitioner was present and had the opportunity to participate in the proceedings; the validity of an oral divorce under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance does not require prior notice if the petitioner acknowledges it.
