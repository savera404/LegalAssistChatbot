**Case Name:**  
AMANULLAH v. SADIA SHAH and others (Constitutional Petitions Nos.884 and 885 of 2012)

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan

**Year:**  
2017

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the respondent (Sadia Shah) is entitled to a dower amount of Rs. 500,000 and to maintenance of her daughter Aliza, and whether the appellate court’s findings on these matters are correct.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Family Courts Act (XXXV of 1964), Section 5, Schedule  
- Mohammedan Law, Para 370 (maintenance of a daughter until marriage)  
- Constitutional provisions on family law (implied)

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Petitioner filed a suit for recovery of dower amount and maintenance of wife and daughter.  
2. Trial court held that the dower of Rs. 500,000 had been paid in the form of property transferred to the respondent and that the respondent was entitled only to maintenance during the iddat period; maintenance for minor children was fixed.  
3. Appellate court reversed the dower finding, directed payment of Rs. 500,000, and modified maintenance orders.  
4. Constitutional petitions challenged the appellate court’s decisions, arguing that the divorce deed (admitted but not produced) and the property transfer constituted payment of dower.

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Supreme Court set aside the appellate court’s findings on issues 2 and 3, upheld the trial court’s findings that the dower was paid in property, and modified the maintenance order for daughter Aliza to be payable until her marriage rather than until majority.

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Admitted documents such as a divorce deed, even if not produced in evidence, are sufficient to establish facts; non‑production does not negate the rights of a party. Payment of dower in the form of property is valid under the Family Courts Act. Under Mohammedan Law, a daughter is entitled to maintenance only until her marriage.
