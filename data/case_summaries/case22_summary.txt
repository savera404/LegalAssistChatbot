**Case Name:**  
FAQIR BAKHSH AND OTHERS—APPELLANTS VERSUS JINDWADDA AND OTHERS—RESPONDENTS  

**Court:**  
Lahore High Court  

**Year:**  
2005  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the mutations (land title changes) effected in favour of Ameer Bakhsh and his heirs, which excluded Mst. Kundan (daughter of Fazal Khan), were void under Islamic inheritance law, and whether the respondents’ suit was barred by Order XXIII, rule 1 of the Civil Procedure Code (CPC) due to the earlier withdrawal of a similar suit.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Civil Procedure Code (1908), Order XXIII, rule 1  
- R.I. Specific Relief Act (1877), Section 42  
- Islamic law (Sharia) governing inheritance  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Agricultural land in Mouza Wari Azeem Khan was left by Bahadur Khan and mutated to his sons Fazal Khan, Karim Bakhsh, and Allah Ditta.  
2. Karim Bakhsh and Allah Ditta died without issue; their shares passed to Fazal Khan, making him sole owner.  
3. Upon Fazal Khan’s death, his son Ameer Bakhsh (predecessor‑in‑interest of the petitioners) obtained mutation No. 797 (24‑9‑1925) in his favour, excluding Mst. Kundan (daughter of Fazal Khan).  
4. Respondents, heirs of Mst. Kundan, filed a suit in 1998 claiming a one‑third share and challenging the validity of the mutations.  
5. Petitioners argued the mutations were made on false records and were void; respondents claimed the suit was barred by the earlier withdrawal of a similar suit (dated 29‑10‑1997, withdrawn 27‑1‑1999).  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Lahore High Court held that:  
- The mutations in favour of Ameer Bakhsh and his heirs were void because they were made in contravention of Islamic inheritance law, which does not permit exclusion of a daughter.  
- The respondents’ suit was not barred by Order XXIII, rule 1 CPC; the earlier suit had been withdrawn unconditionally, and the present suit was filed within the permissible period.  
- The suit was not time‑barred, as the wrong of improper mutation continued to affect the respondents’ rights.  
- Custom excluding women from inheritance was deemed repugnant to Islamic law and could not be invoked.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Mutations that contravene Islamic inheritance provisions are void and ineffective; a suit challenging such mutations is maintainable even after an earlier suit has been withdrawn, and the bar under Order XXIII, rule 1 CPC does not apply in such circumstances. Custom that excludes women from inheritance is not a valid basis for inheritance claims under Sharia.
