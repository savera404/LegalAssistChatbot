**Case Name:**  
Wajiha Rasheed v. Adeel Akhter et al.  

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan (Appellate Jurisdiction)  

**Year:**  
2025  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the petitioner, after a dissolution of marriage by khula in which she failed to prove cruelty, is entitled to retain the dower (including Plot No. 6) that had previously been declared hers by a family‑court decree.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- West‑Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964 – Section 10 (proviso) and Section 10(4)  
- Punjab Family Courts (Amendment) Act, 2015 – Sections 10(5) and 10(6) (regarding dower in khula)  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Marriage: 05 Apr 2001; rukhsa 28 Jun 2004.  
2. Dower fixed at Rs 10,00,000 and Plot No. 6, Sector‑D, Ghazikot Township, Mansehra.  
3. Respondent’s father sold Plot No. 6 to Riaz Ahmed; petitioner purchased it from father‑in‑law on 20 Dec 2010.  
4. Petitioner filed Suit No. 216/FC 2010 for declaration of ownership of Plot No. 6; decree dated 18 Oct 2011.  
5. Petitioner filed Family Suit No. 45 2012 for dissolution of marriage and recovery of dower; marriage dissolved 08 Nov 2012.  
6. Family court held petitioner did not prove cruelty; ordered her to return dower.  
7. Petitioner appealed; appellate court dismissed.  
8. Petitioner filed writ petitions 548‑A/2012 and 1007/2016; High Court decided 17 Mar 2022, allowing 548‑A/2012 and holding petitioner not entitled to dower due to dissolution by khula.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Supreme Court upheld the High Court’s decision that the petitioner is not entitled to retain the dower, including Plot No. 6, after the dissolution of marriage. The earlier decree declaring ownership of the plot is set aside because the subsequent dissolution order requires the return of dower. The amendments to the Family Courts Act enacted in 2015 are not applied retroactively to the 2012 dissolution decree.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
In a dissolution of marriage by khula, if the wife fails to prove cruelty, she must return the dower; prior decrees granting property as part of dower can be set aside when a later dissolution order requires its return. Amendments to the Family Courts Act are not retroactive to earlier decrees.
