**Case Name:**  
Civil Petition No.2872‑L of 2022 (Petitioner: Naseem Mai and another vs Respondent: Malik Muhammad Shah Aalam and others)

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan (Appellate Jurisdiction)

**Year:**  
2025 (judgment dated 7 July 2025)

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the Supreme Court should grant leave to appeal against the Lahore High Court judgment that set aside the petitioners’ claim for future marriage expenses.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Article 185(3) of the Constitution of Islamic Republic of Pakistan, 1973  
- Article 199 of the Constitution  
- Family Courts Act, 1964 – Section 5 read with Schedule‑I  
- Precedents: *State of Bombay v. United Motors Ltd.*, *State of Orissa v. Ram Chandra*

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Petitioners sued respondent No. 1 for maintenance allowance of Rs 10,000/month and future maintenance, and prayed for marriage expenses of Rs 2,000,000.  
2. Trial court awarded Rs 5,000/month for past maintenance, Rs 10,000/month for future maintenance (with 10 % annual increase), and Rs 1,000,000 for marriage expenses.  
3. Respondent’s appeal to the Lahore High Court was dismissed on 13 Sep 2019.  
4. Respondent filed W.P. No. 63155 (2019); the High Court partially accepted the judgment and set aside the marriage‑expenses award.  
5. Petitioners filed a leave petition under Article 185(3) seeking to challenge the High Court’s decision.

**Holding / Decision:**  
Leave to appeal was denied. The Supreme Court upheld the Lahore High Court’s decision to set aside the petitioners’ claim for marriage expenses, holding that such a claim is speculative and not maintainable.

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Courts cannot enforce speculative or indeterminate future marriage expenses; claims must be based on concrete, immediate, and certain rights. Under Article 185(3), leave to appeal is not granted for premature or uncertain claims.
