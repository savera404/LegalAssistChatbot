**Case Name:** MST. RABLA SULTANA v. RIAZ AHMAD AND ANOTHER  

**Court:** Federal Shariat Court  

**Year:** 1992  

**Legal Issue:** Whether the appellant is guilty of Qazf (unlawful sexual intercourse) under Section 7 of the Offence of Qazf (Enforcement of Hadd) Ordinance (VIII of 1979) as alleged by the respondents.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Offence of Qazf (Enforcement of Hadd) Ordinance (VIII of 1979) – Section 7  
- Offence of Zina (Enforcement of Hudood) Ordinance (VIII of 1979) – Sections 10, 19, 8/4/25  
- Section 315/34, P.P.C.  
- Section 265‑H, Cr.P.C.  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Appellant married respondent No. 1 on 11‑5‑1984.  
- Respondent No. 1 alleged that the appellant was pregnant at the time of marriage due to prior illicit intercourse with a person named “Sarsa.”  
- Respondent No. 1 filed a complaint under the Offence of Zina Ordinance in 1988; all accused were acquitted.  
- In 1989 the appellant filed a complaint under Section 7 of the Qazf Ordinance alleging that respondents committed Qazf; respondents denied the allegations and had earlier statements in a family suit.  
- The Federal Shariat Court is hearing an appeal against the acquittal in the 1989 complaint.  

**Holding / Decision:** Not specified.  

**Legal Principle Established:** Not specified.
