**Case Name:**  
Saeed Alam v. Family Judge Rohri (Constitution Petition No. 2875 of 2012)

**Court:**  
Sindh High Court

**Year:**  
2013

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether a Family Court may grant a decree of dissolution by Khula without restoring dower (Haq Mahr) that was paid after the marriage, and whether the proviso to section 10(4) of the West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964, requires restoration of dower paid at the time of marriage only.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964 – section 10(4) and its proviso  
- Muslim Family Law Ordinance, 1961 – Form II, column 15 (dower at time of marriage)  
- Constitution of Pakistan – Article 199 (constitutional jurisdiction)

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Saeed Alam married Mst. Jamila on 3 Jan 2010; dower of Rs 100,000 was deferred.  
- Mst. Jamila filed a suit for dissolution and dowry recovery, alleging abuse and that the dower had not been paid.  
- Saeed Alam claimed the dower was paid in a FAISLA after the marriage.  
- The Family Judge Rohri dissolved the marriage on 1 Sep 2012 under Khula, without ordering restoration of the dower.  
- Saeed Alam filed a constitutional petition under Article 199 challenging the order.

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Sindh High Court held that the proviso to section 10(4) applies only to dower received at the time of marriage. Since the dower in this case was paid later in a FAISLA, it is not covered by the proviso; therefore the Family Court was correct not to restore it. The Court also emphasized that a dispute over dower must be resolved before a decree of dissolution can be issued.

**Legal Principle Established:**  
In a dissolution by Khula, the Family Court must restore to the husband the dower received by the wife at the time of marriage; dower paid after the marriage is not subject to restoration under the proviso to section 10(4) of the West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964.
