**Case Name:**  
Riaz Ahmad and 5 Others v. Faqir Ahmad Khan  

**Court:**  
Peshawar High Court  

**Year:**  
2015  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the petitioners are entitled to a declaration and possession of 600 Kanal land, and whether the trial‑court judgment granting such relief is valid, in light of the inheritance mutation, a subsequent gift mutation, and alleged customary practice (Riwaj).  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- West Pakistan Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1962 – Sections 2, 2‑A, 3(2)  
- North West Frontier Province Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1935  
- Specific Relief Act, 1877 – Sections 3, 5, 42  
- Jurisprudence: 2004 SCMR 1391(a); 2010 CLC 631; 1993 Peshawar 127; 1984 SC 394; 2011 Peshawar 41; 2012 YLR 2710  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioners are heirs of Mst. Zakia, daughter of Mst. Roman Sheda, sister of respondent Faqir Ahmad Khan.  
- The land (600 Kanal) was inherited from Nawab Khan.  
- Respondent transferred 20 Kanal to Mst. Roman Sheda via a gift mutation dated 19 Apr 1955.  
- Petitioners contend the gift mutation was invalid under Islamic inheritance law and that the revenue records are erroneous.  
- Trial court granted the petitioners’ suit for declaration and possession.  
- Appellate court set aside the trial‑court judgment and dismissed the suit.
