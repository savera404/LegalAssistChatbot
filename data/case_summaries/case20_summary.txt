**Case Name:**  
Riaz Ahmad and 5 Others v. Faqir Ahmad Khan  

**Court:**  
Peshawar High Court  

**Year:**  
2015  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the petitioners are entitled to a declaration of ownership and possession of 600 Kanal land and whether the appellate court correctly set aside the trial court’s decree and dismissed the suit.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Islamic inheritance provisions (Qur’an & Sunnah)  
- West Pakistan Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1962 (S. 2, 2‑A, 3(2))  
- North West Frontier Province Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1935 (S. 2, 2‑A, 3(2))  
- Specific Relief Act, 1877 (S. 3, 5, 42)  
- Case law: 2004 SCMR 1391(a), 2010 CLC 631, Atta Muhammad v. Nasiruddin, Muzaffar Khan v. Mst. Roshan Jan, Mirza Muhammad v. Muhammad Shfrin, Abdur Rehman v. Mosam Khan.  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioners claim inheritance of 600 Kanal land from deceased Mst. Zakia, legal heirs of Nawab Khan.  
- Nawab Khan died 1932; his children were Faqir Ahmad (respondent) and Mst. Roman Sheda.  
- Mst. Roman Sheda married, moved to Faqir Ahmad’s house, and gave birth to Mst. Zakia.  
- Inheritance mutation No. 392 (26‑3‑1932) was attested in favour of Faqir Ahmad.  
- Gift mutation No. 4190 (19‑4‑1955) transferred 20 Kanal to Mst. Roman Sheda.  
- Petitioners filed suit on 13‑3‑2007 after Mst. Zakia’s death (2006).  
- Trial court granted decree in favour of petitioners; appellate court set aside the decree and dismissed the suit.
