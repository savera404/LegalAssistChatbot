**Case Name:**  
Constitution Petition No. 24 of 2012 (National Commission on Status of Women v. Government of Pakistan) & Civil Petition No. 773‑P of 2018 (National Commission on Status of Women v. Government of Pakistan)

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan

**Year:**  
2018 (hearing held 31 December 2018; judgment date not specified in the excerpt)

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether informal tribal bodies such as jirgas and panchayats that act as adjudicatory courts are lawful under Pakistani law and international commitments.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Constitution of Pakistan (Articles 4, 8, 9, 10‑A, 14, 25, 34, 37, 33, 37; Article 184(3))  
- International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (Articles 2, 26)  
- Universal Declaration of Human Rights (Articles 7, 8)  
- Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW)  
- Case law cited: *Mst. Shazia v. Station House Officer* (PCrLJ 2004), *Mst. Rahmat Bibi v. Station House Officer* (PLD 2016), *Government of Balochistan v. Azizullah Memon* (PLD 1993)

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioners (NCSW and allied human‑rights activists) challenged the legality of jirgas/panchayats that function as courts.  
- Respondents were the Government of Pakistan.  
- Petitioners alleged that such bodies violate constitutional rights (Articles 4, 8, 9, 10‑A, 14, 25, 34, 37) and international obligations (CEDAW, UDHR, ICCPR).  
- The petition sought declaration that these bodies are illegal, unconstitutional, and that their orders be voided.
