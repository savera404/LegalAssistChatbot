**Case Name:**  
Abida Siddique v. The State and 02 others  

**Court:**  
Lahore High Court  

**Year:**  
2025  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether criminal proceedings against the petitioner should be stayed pending the outcome of related civil/family litigation concerning a forged Nikah Nama.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Sections 420, 467, 468, 471 of the Pakistan Penal Code (PPC)  
- Section 5(2) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- FIR No. 284/2021 (dated 22‑07‑2021) alleges offences under the above sections and the Prevention of Corruption Act.  
- The accused, Fakhar Khalid (Respondent No. 3), is accused of forging a Nikah Nama to claim property left by his deceased father, with Abida Siddique named as the alleged wife.  
- The petitioner’s criminal revision petition seeks a stay of the proceedings, which was denied by the Special Judge, Anti‑Corruption, Sahiwal.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Lahore High Court allows the revision petition, directing the trial court to continue the criminal proceedings but to refrain from disposing of the case until the civil/family case concerning the Nikah Nama is decided.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Criminal proceedings may be stayed pending the resolution of related civil/family litigation when the outcome of the civil case is essential to determining criminal liability, to avoid conflicting judgments.
