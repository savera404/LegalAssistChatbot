**Case Name:**  
Muhammad Naseem et al. v. Asghar Ali Tabassum  

**Court:**  
Lahore High Court  

**Year:**  
2025  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the oral gifts/mutations made by the deceased Bashir Ahmed to the respondent, which excluded other heirs, are void under the principle of *zarar* (harm) and Islamic inheritance law.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
Not specified.  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Deceased Bashir Ahmed died 13 Apr 2016.  
- Four property mutations (Nos. 1276, 1502, 1437, 1532) were executed during his lifetime, bearing his thumb impressions, photograph, and witnesses’ attestations.  
- Petitioners are other children of Bashir Ahmed who allege the gifts were fraudulent and intended to deprive them.  
- Respondent was living with the father and provided care; petitioners had severed ties and were involved in litigation against the father.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The petition was dismissed; the court upheld the validity of the oral gifts/mutations.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
A father may lawfully dispose of property to a child who has earned his trust and affection, and such a gift is not void under the principle of *zarar* even if other heirs are excluded, provided there is no evidence of malice or fraud.
