**Case Name:**  
Muhammad Naseem et al. Versus Asghar Ali Tabassum  

**Court:**  
Lahore High Court, Lahore Judicial Department  

**Year:**  
2025  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the oral gifts (mutations) made by the deceased Bashir Ahmed to his son Asghar Ali Tabassum are void under the doctrine of *zarar* (harm) and Islamic principles, and whether the petitioners can challenge them.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
Not specified.  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Deceased Bashir Ahmed died 13 Apr 2016.  
- Four property mutations (Nos. 1276, 1502, 1437, 1532) dated 2012–2014 transferred land and a house to various heirs.  
- Mutations were based on oral gifts, bearing the deceased’s thumb impression and photograph, and attested by witnesses.  
- Respondent (Asghar Ali Tabassum) was a son from the second wife; petitioners are other children from both wives.  
- Petitioners claim the gifts were fraudulent and made to deprive them, invoking the *Liaqat Ali v. Shahnaz Akhtar* precedent.  
- Trial and appellate courts dismissed the petition; the present petition seeks to set aside the gifts.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Lahore High Court dismissed the petition, upholding the validity of the oral gifts and the mutations.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
A father may lawfully dispose of his property to a child who has earned his trust and affection; such a gift is not void under *zarar* or Islamic law absent evidence of malice or bias. The applicability of the *Liaqat Ali* precedent depends on factual similarity and cannot be applied automatically.
