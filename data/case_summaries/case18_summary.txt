**Case Name:** Allah Rakha and Others v. Federation of Pakistan (and others)  
**Court:** Federal Shariat Court  
**Year:** 2000  
**Legal Issue:** Whether Sections 4, 5, 6 and 7 of the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961 are repugnant to the injunctions of Islam under Article 203‑D of the Constitution of Pakistan.  
**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Constitution of Pakistan, 1973 – Articles 203‑B(C), 203‑D, 203‑G  
- Muslim Family Laws Ordinance (VIII of 1961) – Sections 4, 5, 6, 7  
**Key Facts (brief):**  
- A series of Shariat petitions (Nos. 29/1, 32/1, 37/1, 42/1, 13/L, 3/1, 28/I, 6/L, 10/L, 11/L, etc.) were filed under Article 203‑D challenging the validity of the specified sections of the Ordinance.  
- The petitions alleged that these provisions conflict with the injunctions of Islam.  
- The Federal Shariat Court considered the petitions collectively, referencing the historical background of the Ordinance, earlier judicial decisions, and the jurisdictional framework under Article 203‑D.
