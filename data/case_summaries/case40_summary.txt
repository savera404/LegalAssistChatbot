**Case Name:**  
CPLA No.4072 of 2023 (Petition by Mst. Naila vs. Mehran Khan and others)

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan (Appellate Jurisdiction)

**Year:**  
2025

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the petitioner is entitled to recover 11 tolas of gold ornaments allegedly gifted and subsequently snatched by respondent No.1.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
Not specified

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioner filed a suit on 31‑07‑2021 seeking recovery of 11 tolas of gold ornaments gifted by respondent.  
- Petitioner claimed the ornaments were snatched from her in September 2020 when respondent escorted her to her parents’ house.  
- Respondent admitted making the ornaments but denied snatching them.  
- Family court (22‑12‑2022) dismissed the claim; appellate court (27‑03‑2023) allowed it.  
- Lahore High Court (writ petition No.1644/2023) set aside the appellate court’s judgment and restored the family court’s decision.  
- Supreme Court remands the matter to the appellate court for a fresh determination of the petitioner’s entitlement.

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Supreme Court set aside the appellate court’s judgment and decree dated 27‑03‑2023 and remanded the case to the appellate court, directing it to decide the petitioner’s entitlement to the 11 tolas of gold ornaments based on the evidence on record.

**Legal Principle Established:**  
The appellate court must independently re‑evaluate the evidence and decide the entitlement issue; the High Court may not substitute its views for those of the appellate court. The onus remains on the petitioner to prove that the ornaments were snatched.
