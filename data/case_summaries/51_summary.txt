**Case Name:**  
Mansoor Ali vs. Mst. Anam Hussain, et al.

**Court:**  
Lahore High Court, Rawalpindi Bench

**Year:**  
2020

**Legal Issue:**  
1. Whether depreciation of dowry articles may be imposed on a woman when the husband does not admit giving such articles.  
2. Whether a father may be required to pay maintenance beyond his financial means.  
3. Whether a fixed maintenance award for a child may be reduced following an adverse change in the father’s financial circumstances.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
Muslim Personal Law (Shariah) – principles of *nafqah* (maintenance) and *iddat*; general provisions on dowry and maintenance under Pakistani Muslim personal law.

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Marriage on 10 May 2014; family moved to Qatar.  
- Divorce on 12 July 2018.  
- Suit for maintenance and recovery of dower, dowry articles, and gold ornaments filed by respondent 1.  
- Trial Court (04 Oct 2019) awarded Rs. 15,000/month to respondent 1 for her *iddat* period and Rs. 30,000/month to the minor (with 10 % annual increase).  
- Alternate value of dowry articles set at Rs. 500,000; claim for gold ornaments declined.  
- Appeals dismissed (19 Feb 2020).  
- Petitioner now claims his salary has fallen from 14,500 QAR/month to 7,000 QAR/month, arguing the maintenance for the minor is excessive.

**Holding / Decision:**  
- Maintenance awarded to respondent 1 for her *iddat* period is reasonable and upheld.  
- Depreciation of dowry articles cannot be imposed when the husband does not admit giving such articles; the trial court erred in applying depreciation.  
- A father may not be burdened beyond his financial means; maintenance must be proportionate to his resources.  
- A fixed maintenance award may be reduced if the father’s financial circumstances deteriorate.

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Maintenance for a child must be set in proportion to the father’s actual financial resources and may be adjusted downward when those resources decline; depreciation of dowry articles is permissible only when the husband admits having given them.
