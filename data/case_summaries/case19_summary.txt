**Case Name:**  
Muhammad Ayub Khan and another v. Secretary Board of Directors Hashim Khan Trust Quetta and others  

**Court:**  
Balochistan High Court (appeal court)  

**Year:**  
2008 (appeal filed; trial judgment dated 27 May 2008)  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the appellants, as descendants of Bibi Saeeda (a legal heir of Baz Muhammad Khan), are entitled to a declaration of title, cancellation of mutation entries, and recovery of money, notwithstanding the creation of a trust in 1990 and subsequent sale transactions; and whether the suit is maintainable.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- West Pakistan Land Revenue Act (XVII of 1967), Section 52  
- Limitation Act (IX of 1908), Sections 42 and 52  
- Specific Relief Act (I of 1877), Article 120  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Baz Muhammad Khan died 1911, leaving five legal heirs (three sons, two daughters).  
2. The suit property (Khasra Nos. 189, 190, 191, 194) was mutated in 1913 in the names of the three sons, excluding the daughters.  
3. Appellants claim inheritance through their ancestor Bibi Saeeda (daughter of Baz Muhammad Khan).  
4. A trust (Hashim Khan Ghilzai Trust) was executed 30 July 1990, purportedly transferring property and bank accounts to the trust.  
5. Respondents allege the trust is valid and that sale transactions (including Mutation No. 2567) were lawful.  
6. Trial court dismissed the suit for want of merit, holding the trust valid and denying relief to appellants.
