**Case Name:**  
Muhammad Ayub Khan and another v. Secretary Board of Directors Hashim Khan Trust Quetta and others  

**Court:**  
Balochistan High Court (appeal)  

**Year:**  
2008 (appeal filed; trial court judgment dated 27 May 2008)  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the appellants, as descendants of Bibi Saeeda (a legal heir of Baz Muhammad Khan), were entitled to a declaration of title, cancellation of mutation entries, and other reliefs, and whether the trust created by Hashim Khan Ghilzai and subsequent sale transactions were valid.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- West Pakistan Land Revenue Act, 1967 – Section 52  
- Limitation Act, 1908 – Sections 42, 52  
- Specific Relief Act, 1877 – Article 120  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Property: Khasra Nos. 189, 190, 191, 194 (85759 sq ft) in Quetta, inherited from Baz Muhammad Khan (d. 1911).  
- Mutation in 1913 recorded the three sons (Muhammad Qasim, Muhammad Hashim, Muhammad Kazim) as owners; daughters Bibi Wafa and Bibi Saeeda were omitted.  
- Appellants are descendants of Bibi Saeeda.  
- A trust (Hashim Khan Ghilzai Trust) was executed 30 July 1990, purportedly transferring property and bank accounts to the trust.  
- Respondents Nos. 2‑8 sold most of the property; respondents Nos. 9‑27 purchased portions and later sold them.  
- Appellants sought cancellation of mutation entries, invalidation of the trust, recovery of bank deposits, and mesne profits.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The trial court dismissed the suit for want of merit, held the trust to be legally valid, and found the appellants not entitled to the reliefs claimed. The appellate decision is not specified in the excerpt.
