**Case Name:** Hafiz Abdul Waheed v. Miss Asma Jehangir and Another  
**Court:** Lahore High Court  
**Year:** 1997  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether a marriage contracted by a female without the consent of her wali and without a valid Nikah is valid; the enforceability of parents’ obedience; and whether marriage in Islam is a civil contract.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Muslim Family Laws Ordinance (VIII of 1961) – Section 5  
- Constitution of Pakistan (1973) – Article 203‑GG (and related provisions)  
- Islamic jurisprudence principles cited in the judgment  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
Saima Waheed, a 4th‑year student, allegedly entered into a secret marriage with Muhammad Arshad on 26 Feb 1996. Her father discovered the marriage on 9 Mar 1996, obtained a Nikahnama stating no Nikah had been performed, and later learned that Saima was detained in a Dastak. The father filed Criminal Miscellaneous No. 425‑H of 1996, a habeas corpus petition, and a writ petition, raising the issues above.  

**Holding / Decision:** Not specified.  

**Legal Principle Established:** Not specified.
