**Case Name:** Muhammad Irfan vs. Additional District Judge, Ferozwala, District Sheikhupura and others  
**Court:** Supreme Court of Pakistan  
**Year:** 2025  

**Legal Issue:** Whether medical treatment for a wife and minor child is included within the definition of “maintenance” under Muslim family law.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:** Section 369 of the Muhammadan Law (as cited in the judgment).  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioner, Muhammad Irfan, filed a suit seeking maintenance, dowry articles, and dissolution of marriage against Respondent No. 3 (his wife) and Respondent No. 4 (their minor son).  
- The High Court increased the maintenance allowance for the minor and medical expenses, and the Supreme Court was approached on appeal.  
- Petitioner argued that “medical treatment” is not covered by the term “maintenance.”  

**Holding / Decision:** The Supreme Court dismissed the petition, affirming that medical treatment is part of maintenance.  

**Legal Principle Established:** Maintenance under Muslim family law is inclusive and not limited by a cap; it encompasses medical treatment for a wife and minor child.
