**Case Name:** Muhammad Irfan vs. Additional District Judge, Ferozwala, District Sheikhupura and others  
**Court:** Supreme Court of Pakistan  
**Year:** 2025  
**Legal Issue:** Whether medical treatment is encompassed within the definition of “maintenance” under Muslim family law.  
**Relevant Law / Sections:** Section 369 of the Muhammadan Law (as cited in the judgment).  
**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioner sued for maintenance allowance, dowry articles, and dissolution of marriage.  
- Respondent No. 3 (petitioner’s wife) and minor son sought maintenance; court awarded monthly maintenance, dowry, delivery expenses, and medical treatment costs.  
- Respondent No. 3 appealed, arguing that medical treatment is not covered by “maintenance.”  
- The Supreme Court dismissed the appeal.  
**Holding / Decision:** The Supreme Court dismissed the appeal, affirming that medical treatment is included in the scope of maintenance.  
**Legal Principle Established:** Maintenance under Muslim family law is inclusive and not limited by a cap; it covers medical treatment for the wife and minor children.
