**Case Name:**  
In re: MST. ZAINAB AND OTHERS (PETITIONERS)

**Court:**  
Sindh High Court

**Year:**  
1986

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether a widow who is the sole surviving heir of a Muslim husband is entitled to the entire estate (including the residue) under Muslim personal law, and how the doctrine of “Return” applies to Shia and Sunni inheritance rules.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
Muslim Personal Law (Sharia) – inheritance provisions; doctrine of Return; Shia (Imamiyah, Zaidiyyah) and Sunni jurisprudence on widow’s share and residue.

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Three petitions (S. M. A. No. 73 of 1983, No. 43 of 1984, No. 25 of 1985) were filed by widows claiming sole inheritance of their deceased husbands.  
- In the first case the parties were Shia; in the other two they were Sunni.  
- The court heard arguments from the petitioners’ counsel, Amicus Curiae, and the Advocate‑General, and considered numerous classical and contemporary sources on Shia and Sunni inheritance law.
