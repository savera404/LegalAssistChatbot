**Case Name:** In re: MST. ZAINAB AND OTHERS (PETITIONERS)  
**Court:** Sindh High Court  
**Year:** 1986  
**Legal Issue:** Whether a widow who is the sole surviving heir of her deceased husband is entitled to the entire estate under Muslim inheritance law.  
**Relevant Law / Sections:** Islamic law of inheritance (Sharia), doctrine of return, Shia and Sunni jurisprudence on widow’s share.  
**Key Facts (brief):**  
- The widow, together with two other petitioners, claimed to be the sole heirs of their deceased husbands.  
- The petitions were filed under S. M. A. No. 73 of 1983, No. 43 of 1984, and No. 25 of 1985.  
- The parties presented arguments from both Shia and Sunni perspectives, with no other heirs named.  
- Amicus curiae and the Advocate‑General appeared to support the petitioners’ claims.  
**Holding / Decision:** The court held that, in the absence of any other heirs, the widow is entitled to the entire estate by way of return, following the modern juristic view.  
**Legal Principle Established:** The doctrine of return applies to a widow who is the sole surviving heir, entitling her to the entire estate under Muslim inheritance law.
