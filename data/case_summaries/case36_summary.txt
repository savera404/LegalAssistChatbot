**Case Name:**  
Ehsan‑ul‑Haq and others v. Muhammad Nawaz and others  

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan (Appellate Jurisdiction)  

**Year:**  
2025  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether a condition limiting the ownership of a property in a will is void, and consequently whether the legal heirs of the nephew of the original owner are entitled to a share in the property.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1962 – Section 2‑A (recognition of male heirs inheriting under customary law before 15 March 1948 as absolute owners).  
- Islamic inheritance principles (one‑third rule for testamentary dispositions).  
- Customary law as applied to ancestral property (not to acquired land).  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Kala Khan owned land in Jhelum; died 1934.  
2. His will left the land to his wife Mst. Jewani and daughter Mst. Ghulam Zohra, with a condition that she be a limited owner until death.  
3. Mst. Ghulam Zohra died 1988; the property was mutated in favour of her husband and children.  
4. The appellants are legal heirs of Muhammad Yousaf (Kala Khan’s nephew). They claim that, after Mst. Ghulam Zohra’s death, half the property should devolve to them.  
5. Trial court granted the claim; appellate court dismissed it; the Supreme Court is hearing the appeal.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Supreme Court held that the condition of limited ownership in the will was void under Islamic inheritance law; Mst. Ghulam Zohra became the absolute owner of the property. Upon her death, the property devolved to her legal heirs, not to the appellants.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
A condition that limits a beneficiary’s ownership in a will is void if it conflicts with the one‑third rule of Islamic inheritance; customary law does not apply to land acquired during the owner’s lifetime, and male heirs who inherited under customary law before 1948 are recognized as absolute owners under Islamic law.
