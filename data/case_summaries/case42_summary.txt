**Case Name:**  
MANZOOR AHMAD v. CHIRAGH KHAN (deceased) through LRs  

**Court:**  
Lahore High Court (Civil Revision)  

**Year:**  
2025  

**Legal Issue:**  
1. Whether the respondents are entitled to a declaration regarding the suit land.  
2. Whether inheritance mutation No.294 dated 20.07.2002 is void on the ground of fraud or error.  
3. Whether the death certificate of Mst. Nooran is forged and thus invalid.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Article 227 of the Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan (principles of the Holy Quran and Sunnah).  
- Islamic inheritance provisions under the Quran and Sunnah (as cited in the judgment).  
- (Civil Procedure Code provisions applicable to civil revision – not specified).  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Noora, son of Makhna, had a daughter Mst. Nooran.  
- After Noora’s death (1940), his widow Mst. Sammo married Rustam (Noora’s brother), producing Manzoor Ahmad (petitioner).  
- Mst. Nooran died (date disputed: 14.10.2001 vs. 10.06.1998).  
- Inheritance mutation No.294 dated 20.07.2002 granted the property to Manzoor Ahmad.  
- Respondents (Chiragh Khan, Zahoor Ahmad, etc.) filed a suit for declaration claiming entitlement to the property and challenging the mutation.  
- The suits were consolidated; the trial court (Civil Judge, Kamalia) decreed in favour of the respondents and dismissed the petitioner’s suit.  
- The petitioner appealed to the Addl. District Judge, Pir Mahal, whose judgment (dated 11.12.2017) dismissed the appeal.
