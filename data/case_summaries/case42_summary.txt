**Case Name:**  
MANZOOR AHMAD v. CHIRAGH KHAN (deceased) through LRs  

**Court:**  
High Court of Lahore  

**Year:**  
2025  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the respondents (sons of Rustam) are entitled to a declaration that they inherit a share of Mst. Nooran’s estate and that the inheritance mutation No.294 dated 20.07.2002 in favour of Manzoor Ahmad is void; and whether the death certificate of Mst. Nooran is forged.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Islamic inheritance principles as per the Quran and Sunnah (Surah An-Nisa, Ayat 7‑11, 176)  
- Article 227 of the Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, 1973 (provisions relating to the Holy Quran and Sunnah)  
- Civil Procedure Code (suits for declaration)  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Noora, son of Makhna, had a daughter Mst. Nooran.  
- After Noora’s death (1940), his widow Sammo married Rustam (Noora’s brother), producing Manzoor Ahmad (uterine brother of Nooran).  
- Mst. Nooran died on 14.10.2001.  
- Inheritance mutation No.294 dated 20.07.2002 granted the property to Manzoor Ahmad.  
- Respondents (Chiragh Khan, Zahoor Ahmad, etc.) claim they are sons of Rustam and thus entitled to a share, and that the death certificate of Nooran is forged.  
- Petitioner (Manzoor Ahmad) filed a suit for declaration asserting his right as the sole heir; the trial court dismissed his suit and granted the respondents’ suit.
