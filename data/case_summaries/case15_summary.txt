**Case Name:** Shakeel Ahmad v. Additional District Judge, Lahore and Another  
**Court:** Lahore High Court  
**Year:** 2008  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the Family Court at Lahore had jurisdiction to entertain a suit for recovery of dowry articles filed by respondent No. 2 on 26‑10‑2001, before the amendment that added “dowry” to the schedule of the West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964 – Sections 5, 7(2) and the Schedule  
- West Pakistan Family Court Rules, 1965 – Rules 5 and 6  
- Amendment Act VII of 1997 to the Act (incorporating “dowry” into the schedule)  
- Constitution of Pakistan, Article 199 (interpretation of statutes)  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Respondent No. 2 filed a suit for recovery of dowry articles on 26‑10‑2001 in the Family Court at Lahore.  
2. The suit was filed before the amendment that added “dowry” to the schedule of the Act.  
3. The Family Court initially found no jurisdiction; the appellate court reversed that finding and remanded the case for further determination.  
4. The petitioner challenged the appellate court’s decision, arguing the suit was not competent because it was filed before the amendment.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Lahore High Court dismissed the petition, affirming that the suit was within the jurisdiction of the Family Court at Lahore. The court held that the amendment to the schedule of the Act, which added “dowry” as a subject, applied to suits filed before the amendment, and that the ordinary residence of the wife (Lahore) determined territorial jurisdiction, not the location of the dowry articles.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Amendments to the schedule of the West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964, which add new subject matters, apply to suits filed before the amendment, and the ordinary residence of the wife governs the territorial jurisdiction of the Family Court, irrespective of the location of the dowry articles.
