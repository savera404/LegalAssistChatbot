**Case Name:**  
Riaz Javed v. Sheraz Ahmed and 4 Others  

**Court:**  
Lahore High Court  

**Year:**  
2010  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the Family Courts, under Section 5 of the West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964, have jurisdiction to entertain matters involving non‑Muslims (specifically Ahmadis) when the Act contains a “subject to” clause referring to the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964 – Section 5(1)  
- Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961 – Section 1(2)  
- Constitution of Pakistan, 1973 – Article 199; Article 260(3)(b)  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Respondents filed a suit for maintenance, dower, dowry, dissolution of marriage and visitation before the Family Court, Sialkot.  
2. Petitioner objected to the court’s jurisdiction, arguing that Section 5 of the Act does not apply to non‑Muslims.  
3. Amicus curiae (Mirza Adeel Ahmed) and counsel for respondents argued that the “subject to” clause is religion‑neutral and that the MFLO is not applicable to non‑Muslims.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Lahore High Court held that:  
- The “subject to” clause in Section 5 is a conflict‑resolution device; it is operative only when there is a conflict between the MFLO and the Act.  
- The MFLO applies solely to Muslim citizens of Pakistan; therefore, it does not apply to non‑Muslims, including Ahmadis.  
- Consequently, the “subject to” clause has no effect on the jurisdiction of the Family Court over non‑Muslims.  
- The Family Court has exclusive jurisdiction to entertain matters specified in Part I of the Schedule for non‑Muslims, applying the appropriate personal law.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Section 5(1) of the West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964, is religion‑neutral; the “subject to” clause merely determines which law prevails in case of conflict. It does not restrict the Family Court’s jurisdiction over non‑Muslims, allowing such cases to be heard under the relevant personal law.
