**Case Name:** Ibrar vs The State etc.  
**Court:** Lahore High Court, Lahore (Judicial Department)  
**Year:** 2025  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the conviction and death sentence imposed on Ibrar under Sections 302 and 324 of the Pakistan Penal Code (PPC) are viable, and whether the delay in filing the First Information Report (FIR) affects the admissibility of the evidence.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Section 302 PPC (Murder)  
- Section 324 PPC (Use of Firearm)  
- Section 544‑A Cr.P.C. (Compensation)  
- Section 173 Cr.P.C. (Investigation)  
- Section 342 Cr.P.C. (Statement of Accused)  
- Section 340(2) Cr.P.C. (Oath of Accused)  
- Section 161 Cr.P.C. (Witness Statement)  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- On 17 Jan 2020, Ibrar fired a 30‑bore pistol at his daughter‑in‑law, Zeenat Bibi, killing her; he also shot an injured witness.  
- He fled the scene; the police recovered the pistol and live bullets.  
- Zeenat Bibi died on 24 Feb 2020; Section 302 was added to the charge.  
- Investigation produced ocular, medical, forensic, and circumstantial evidence.  
- The FIR was lodged on 18 Jan 2020, approximately 30 hours after the incident.  
- Ibrar pleaded not guilty, did not give a statement under Section 340(2), and produced no defence evidence.  
- The trial court convicted him and imposed a death sentence, plus imprisonment and compensation.  
- A Murder Reference (No. 74/2021) was filed for confirmation of the death sentence.
