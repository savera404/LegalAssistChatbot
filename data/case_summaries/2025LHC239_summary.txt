**Case Name:** Ibrar vs The State  
**Court:** Lahore High Court, Judicial Department  
**Year:** 2025 (judgment dated 28 January 2025)  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the conviction and death sentence imposed on Ibrar under Sections 302 and 324 of the Pakistan Penal Code (PPC) are viable, and whether the delay in filing the First Information Report (FIR) affects the admissibility of evidence.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Section 302 PPC (Murder)  
- Section 324 PPC (Use of Firearm)  
- Section 544‑A Cr.P.C. (Compensation)  
- Section 173 Cr.P.C. (Investigation)  
- Section 342 Cr.P.C. (Statement of Accused)  
- Section 340(2) Cr.P.C. (Oath)  
- Section 161 Cr.P.C. (Witness)  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- On 17 January 2020, Ibrar, armed with a 30‑bore pistol, fired at his daughter‑in‑law Zeenat Bibi, killing her, and also shot Asghar Ali, who was injured.  
- Ibrar fled the scene; the pistol and bullets were recovered during investigation.  
- The case was registered as FIR No. 77/2020; the FIR was lodged on 18 January 2020.  
- Ibrar was convicted by the Additional Sessions Judge, Gujranwala, and sentenced to death under Section 302 PPC, with additional imprisonment and fines under Section 324 PPC.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
Not specified in the provided excerpt.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
- A delay in lodging an FIR is not per se fatal to a case; it does not invalidate reliable ocular or circumstantial evidence.  
- Delay is only relevant in cases involving enmity; absent evidence of enmity, the delay does not prejudice the prosecution.  
- The court reiterated that the presence of credible eyewitness testimony and forensic evidence can sustain a conviction even when the FIR is filed after a delay.
