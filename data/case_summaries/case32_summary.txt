**Case Name:**  
Mst. RAMZANU BIBI v. IBRAHIM (deceased) through L.Rs. and others  

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan  

**Year:**  
2025  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the mutation dated 24 September 1974 (No. 914) recording an oral gift of land from Ramzanu Bibi to Ibrahim and Shadi is valid, and whether the appellant is entitled to the title of the land inherited from her mother.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Pakistani property law governing mutation and transfer of land (Transfer of Property Act, 1882 – not explicitly cited).  
- Principles of fraudulent conveyance and the burden of proof in civil claims.  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Ramzanu Bibi inherited land from her mother Mst. Jimmy.  
- Mutation No. 913 (inheritance) transferred the land to Ramzanu Bibi but did not record her presence.  
- Mutation No. 914 recorded an oral gift of the land to Ibrahim and Shadi, with Ramzanu Bibi’s thumb impression.  
- The appellant challenged the gift mutation before the Collector and later in civil court, alleging fraud and lack of evidence.  
- Respondents failed to disclose essential details of the alleged gift in their written statements and did not provide sufficient evidence.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Supreme Court held that the gift mutation No. 914 is void ab initio due to lack of proof of a valid gift and evidence of fraud. The appellant is entitled to the title of the land.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
A mutation does not confer title; the burden of proving a valid gift lies with the parties asserting the mutation. Failure to meet pleading, evidentiary, and persuasion burdens, coupled with evidence of fraud, renders the mutation void.
