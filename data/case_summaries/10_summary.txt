**Case Name:** Crl. Misc. No. 17119‑B/2014  
**Court:** Not specified  
**Year:** 2015  

**Legal Issue:** Whether the petitioner, Umer Din, is entitled to post‑arrest bail on the statutory ground under Section 497 Cr.P.C. after more than two years of arrest in an honor‑killing case.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Section 497 Cr.P.C. (post‑arrest bail on statutory ground)  
- Sections 302, 148, 149, 109 of the Pakistan Penal Code (charges in the FIR)  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioner Umer Din was arrested on 08 Oct 2012 under FIR No. 277 for murder (Section 302) and related offences.  
- The charge was framed on 02 Jan 2013; the trial is ongoing.  
- The alleged offence is an honor killing: the victim was the petitioner’s daughter, killed at the petitioner’s house.  
- The petitioner seeks post‑arrest bail on the statutory ground, arguing he is not a hardened, desperate, or dangerous criminal.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The petition is dismissed; post‑arrest bail on the statutory ground is denied.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Under Section 497 Cr.P.C., an accused is not entitled to post‑arrest bail on the statutory ground if the court finds the accused to be a hardened, desperate, or dangerous criminal. In honor‑killing cases, the court may interpret these terms broadly to prevent such crimes and protect societal interests.
