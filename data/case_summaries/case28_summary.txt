**Case Name:**  
Civil Petition No. 5009 of 2024 (Petitioner: Muhammad Imran Baqir vs. Mst. Zarnain Arzoo & others)

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan (Appellate Jurisdiction)

**Year:**  
2025

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the Supreme Court should grant leave to appeal against the High Court’s dismissal of a Constitution Petition concerning the quantum of maintenance allowance for a minor son.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Article 185(3) of the Constitution of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, 1973  
- Islamic jurisprudence on maintenance (Nasab)  
- Case law: *Humayun Hassan v. Arsalan Hassan* (PLD 2013 SC 557)  
- Other Islamic legal principles cited in the judgment (no specific statutory sections specified)

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Petitioner was previously married to respondent Mst. Zarnain Arzoo.  
2. Respondent sought dower, maintenance for herself, and maintenance for her minor son, Ameer Haider Khan.  
3. Trial court (20 Nov 2021) awarded maintenance of Rs. 25,000 /‑ per month for the son with a 10 % annual increase until majority.  
4. Appellate court (06 Apr 2022) reduced the maintenance awarded to respondent No. 1 but upheld the son’s maintenance award.  
5. Petitioner filed a Constitution Petition, which was dismissed by the Lahore High Court (03 Oct 2024).  
6. Petitioner now seeks leave to appeal to the Supreme Court.

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Supreme Court dismissed the petition and declined leave to appeal, thereby upholding the findings of the lower courts.

**Legal Principle Established:**  
The father’s obligation to provide maintenance for a minor child is independent of the dissolution of marriage and persists until the child attains majority; the Supreme Court will not interfere with lower courts’ determinations of maintenance quantum absent clear error.
