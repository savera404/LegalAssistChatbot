**Case Name:**  
Civil Petition Nos. 768 and 827 of 2022 (Mst. Fakhra Jabeen v. Wasif Ali and others)

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan (Appellate Jurisdiction)

**Year:**  
2024

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the entries in columns 13 (cash dower) and 16 (property dower) of a Nikahnama are to be read separately or conjunctively, and whether a dower described in column 16 becomes operative only if the cash amount in column 13 is unpaid; also whether a person not privy to the Nikahnama can be bound by its entries.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Article 185(3) of the Constitution of Pakistan  
- Article 199 of the Constitution of Pakistan  
- Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961  
- West Pakistan Rules under the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Marriage contracted on 05 Jun 2005; Nikahnama executed and registered.  
- Dower recorded: Rs 500,000 in column 13; a plot described in column 16.  
- Disputes over dower and maintenance led to litigation.  
- Trial court declared the certified copy of the Nikahnama (Ex‑P‑2) genuine; the uncertified copy (Ex‑D‑6) was discarded.  
- High Court held that columns 13 and 16 are distinct; the property in column 16 is payable only if the cash in column 13 is not paid, and that entries are to be interpreted in favour of the groom.  
- High Court found respondent No. 2 liable for the obligation in column 16.
