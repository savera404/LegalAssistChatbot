**Case Name:**  
Mst. Farishta v. Federation of Pakistan (through Ministry of Law, Islamabad)

**Court:**  
Peshawar High Court

**Year:**  
1979 (reported 1980 PLD 47)

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether section 4 of the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961—providing that the children of a pre‑deceased son or daughter shall inherit a share equal to that of the deceased parent—contravenes the Injunctions of Islam, and whether the bench has jurisdiction to examine this under Article 203‑B of the Constitution.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Constitution of Pakistan, 1973 – Articles 203‑A to 203‑D (including Article 203‑B and its explanation)  
- Muslim Family Laws Ordinance, 1961 – Section 4  
- Order, 1977 C.M.L.A. No. 21 (Interpretation of Statutes)  
- Article 8(3)(b) of the Constitution (protection of Muslim Personal Law)

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Petitioner filed a petition under Article 203‑B seeking a declaration that section 4 is repugnant to Islam.  
2. The bench heard counsel on 8–9 September 1979 and 22–23 September 1979, including the Advocate‑General and other experts.  
3. The court examined whether the ordinance falls within “Muslim Personal Law” as defined in the explanation to Article 203‑B, and whether statutory provisions can be reviewed for conformity with the Qur’an and Sunnah.
