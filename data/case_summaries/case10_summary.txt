**Case Name:**  
Constitutional Petition No. S‑55 of 1982 (Hayat Khatoon v. Allah Dino and Another)

**Court:**  
Sindh High Court

**Year:**  
1985 (judgment cited as 1985 CLC 1343)

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the family court had jurisdiction to grant custody of minors under the Guardians and Wards Act and whether the order was issued in accordance with the principles of natural justice.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964 – ss 2, 5, 25  
- Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 – s 25  
- Family Court Rules, 1965 – RR 3, 7  
- Constitution of Pakistan (provisions on natural justice) – not specified

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Petitioner Hayat Khatoon was married to Allah Bachayo Pahor (deceased 19‑12‑1978) and had 11 children, 5 of whom were minors.  
2. She was appointed guardian of the five minors by the Civil Judge, Family Court, Shahdadpur (order 21‑5‑1979).  
3. Her eldest sons (Allah Dino, Ghulam Rasool, Shabbir) seized four of the minors, evicted her, and she relocated to Kandiaro where she married Haroon Pahor.  
4. Petitioner filed Civil Suit No. 27/1981 seeking: (a) confirmation of her marriage to Haroon Pahor; (b) restraining interference by the respondents; (c) protection of the minors.  
5. Allah Dino filed a suit under section 25 of the Guardians and Wards Act seeking custody of two minors, claiming he was the appointed guardian per a prior order.  
6. Respondent No. 2 (family judge) granted custody to Allah Dino on 10‑11‑1981, an order the petitioner challenged in the constitutional petition on grounds of lack of jurisdiction and violation of natural justice.
