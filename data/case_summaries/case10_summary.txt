**Case Name:**  
Mst. Hayat Khatoon v. Allah Dino and Another  

**Court:**  
Sindh High Court  

**Year:**  
1984  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the Family Court (Respondent No. 2) had jurisdiction to grant custody of minor children under Section 25 of the Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, and whether the impugned order violated principles of natural justice.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 – Section 25  
- Family Court Rules, 1965 – Rules 3 and 7  
- West Pakistan Family Courts Act, 1964 – Sections 2, 5, 25 and Schedule Item 5  
- Provisional Constitution Order (1 of 1981) – Article 9 (West Pakistan Family Courts)  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Petitioner was married to Allah Bachayo Pahor (deceased 19‑12‑1978) and had 11 children, 5 of whom were minors at the time of his death.  
2. Petitioner was appointed guardian of the five minors by a Civil Judge (order dated 21‑5‑1979).  
3. Eldest sons (Allah Dino, Ghulam Rasool, Shabbir) allegedly seized four of the minors and forced the petitioner to leave the family home; she subsequently remarried Muhammad Haroon.  
4. Petitioner filed Civil Suit No. 27 of 1981 seeking: (a) confirmation of her marriage to Haroon; (b) protection from interference by the sons; (c) custody of the minors.  
5. Respondent No. 1 (Allah Dino) filed a suit under Section 25 of the Guardians and Wards Act seeking custody of two minors (Mst. Hajra and Irshad).  
6. The Family Court (Respondent No. 2) granted custody to Allah Dino on 10‑11‑1981, issuing an order that the minors be produced to the court if required.  
7. Petitioner filed a constitutional petition alleging lack of jurisdiction, inapplicability of Section 25, and violation of natural justice.
