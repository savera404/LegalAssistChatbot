**Case Name:** Muhammad Siddique v. State (Appellant: Muhammad Siddique)  
**Court:** Lahore High Court  
**Year:** 2002  

**Legal Issue:** Whether the appellant is guilty of the triple murder of Mst. Salma Bibi, Muhammad Aslam, and Mst. Saba Saleem, and whether a compromise can be accepted for such offences.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Section 302(b) P.P.C. (murder with intent to kill)  
- Section 302(c) P.P.C. (murder with intent to kill)  
- Section 306(b) P.P.C. (punishment for murder)  
- Section 338‑E P.P.C. (waiver or compounding of offences)  
- Section 544‑A P.P.C. (compensation to heirs)  
- Suppression of Terrorist Activities (Special Courts) Act, 1975  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- The appellant and his son were tried under the S.T.A. Act for the triple murder of Mst. Salma Bibi (18/20 yrs), Muhammad Aslam (25/30 yrs), and Mst. Saba Saleem (6/7 mo).  
- FIR No. 249 (12‑9‑1995) registered under section 302/34 P.P.C. at Ladheywala Warraich Police Station.  
- Evidence included a recovered .12‑bore gun, blood‑stained earth, post‑mortem reports, and statements of eight witnesses (including the father and brother of one deceased).  
- The appellant denied the prosecution’s story, claiming no independent witnesses and no crime‑empty recovered.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Lahore High Court upheld the trial court’s conviction, rejecting the compromise as an admission of guilt, and affirmed the sentences imposed (death as Tazir for one murder and 25 years R.I. for the other two, with concurrent execution).  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
A compromise is an admission of guilt and cannot be accepted for murder; the court must rely on the totality of evidence (weapon, bodies, witness testimony, post‑mortem) to determine guilt beyond reasonable doubt.
