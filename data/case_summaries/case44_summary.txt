**Case Name:**  
Writ Petition No. 19809/2013, Mst. Ruqqia Bibi  

**Court:**  
Supreme Court of Pakistan  

**Year:**  
2013  

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the dismissal of the petition for transfer of the case to an ordinary court by the Special Judge Anti‑Terrorism Court, Sargodha, is valid, and whether the offence of “hurt caused by corrosive substance” is a scheduled offence exclusively triable by an Anti‑Terrorism Court.  

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Anti‑Terrorism Act, 1997 – Sections 7, 12; Third Schedule (para (iv))  
- Criminal Law (Second Amendment) Act, 2011 – amendment of P.P.C. Sections 336‑A and 336‑B  
- Pakistan Penal Code, 1860 – Sections 336‑A and 336‑B  
- Section 2(t) of the Anti‑Terrorism Act – definition of “scheduled offence”  

**Key Facts (brief):**  
1. Petitioner, Mst. Ruqqia Bibi, was accused of sprinkling a bottle of acid on Mst. Amina Bibi (second wife of the complainant), causing injuries.  
2. An FIR (No. 162, 25 April 2013) was registered under Section 336‑B of the P.P.C.  
3. Petitioner filed a petition for transfer of the case to an ordinary court; the Special Judge Anti‑Terrorism Court dismissed the petition on 1 July 2013.  
4. Petitioner argued the act was a family dispute and not an act of terrorism; the prosecution maintained it was a scheduled offence under the Anti‑Terrorism Act.  

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Supreme Court dismissed the writ petition, upheld the Special Judge’s order, and held that the offence falls within the ambit of Section 336‑A/B of the P.P.C., which is a scheduled offence under the Anti‑Terrorism Act and is exclusively triable by an Anti‑Terrorism Court.  

**Legal Principle Established:**  
Offences involving hurt caused by corrosive substances (Sections 336‑A/B, P.P.C.) are scheduled offences under the Anti‑Terrorism Act, and Section 12’s use of “only” confers exclusive jurisdiction to Anti‑Terrorism Courts over such cases.
