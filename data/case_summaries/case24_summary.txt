**Case Name:**  
MST. JAMEELA KHATOON AND 4 OTHERS (PETITIONERS) VERSUS SATTAR BAIG AND 2 OTHERS (RESPONDENTS)

**Court:**  
Lahore High Court

**Year:**  
1997

**Legal Issue:**  
Whether the petitioners are owners in possession of the disputed land and whether the mutations and decrees affecting the title are valid; related issues of adverse possession, special costs, and estoppel.

**Relevant Law / Sections:**  
- Civil Procedure Code (1908) s.12(2)  
- West Pakistan Rehabilitation and Settlement Scheme, 1956 – paras. 46, 46‑A, 67  
- West Pakistan Land Revenue Act (XVII of 1967) – s.42  
- Qanun‑e‑Shahadat (10 of 1984) – Articles 64, 71, 88, 101, 119

**Key Facts (brief):**  
- Petitioners claimed ownership of 2143 Kanals land in Jhang as heirs of Mst. Badar‑un‑Nisa (deceased 1947/1952).  
- Respondents challenged the petitioners’ title, contesting mutations (Nos. 1106, 1115, 976, 1133) and civil‑court decrees (1981, 1982).  
- Petitioners alleged fraud and misrepresentation in the creation of those instruments.  
- Trial court held petitioners were owners; appellate court reversed that finding, concluding petitioners had no legitimate relation to the deceased and were not entitled to inheritance.  
- Revision petition was filed in the Lahore High Court.

**Holding / Decision:**  
The Lahore High Court reversed the appellate court’s findings on Issues 1, 2 and 15, holding that the petitioners were not owners of the land. It also reversed the special‑costs findings, ruling that the respondents were entitled to the title. The petitioners’ claims were dismissed.

**Legal Principle Established:**  
A court may set aside earlier mutations and decrees if evidence shows the parties claiming inheritance lack a legitimate relationship to the deceased; inheritance rights are not conferred by mere association. Special costs may be awarded against parties who are not entitled to title.
